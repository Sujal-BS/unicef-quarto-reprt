Vitamin A Supplementation: Saving Children's Lives Worldwide

Sujal Boodgere Sunil - A00096721 | BAA1030 UNICEF Data Analyst Report

1. Executive Summary

Vitamin A deficiency is one of the leading preventable causes of childhood blindness and mortality. Routine supplementation for children aged 6–59 months can reduce under-five mortality by up to 23%.

This report analyses global coverage using UNICEF data and highlights where urgent action is needed to protect children.

2. Why This Issue Matters

Millions of children still suffer from Vitamin A deficiency, leading to preventable blindness and increased risk of death from infections. Supplementation is a simple and highly cost-effective intervention to protect child health.

3. Key Findings

  • Global coverage has improved since 2000, but large regional disparities remain.
  • Higher GDP per capita is strongly associated with better supplementation coverage.
  • Several countries still have coverage below 10%.
  • Coverage dropped noticeably in 2020 due to COVID-19 disruptions.
  • Low coverage often correlates with higher child mortality in the 5–9 age group.

4. Data & Methodology

Data from UNICEF Indicator 1 and Indicator 2 were merged with World Bank metadata using the **Polars** library in Python. All visualisations were created with the **plotnine** package.

6. Recommendations & Call to Action

To improve child survival rates, the following actions are recommended:

  • Strengthen supply chains for reliable delivery.
  • Integrate supplementation with routine immunisation.
  • Run community awareness campaigns.
  • Provide targeted funding to low-coverage countries.

Every percentage point increase in coverage can save thousands of young lives.
